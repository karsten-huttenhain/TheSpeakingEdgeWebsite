Part Six

Build Long-Term Quiet Influence.

Tactics get you through the next meeting. Habits make you the person the room turns to every time.

Quiet influence is not built in a single meeting. It's built across hundreds of small moments.

  • Be slightly under-busy on purpose — Have time to think.
  • Ship one careful written artefact a month — A memo, brief, or position paper.
  • Develop a public point of view — Stake out a clear, considered position on something your team cares about.
  • Be reliable about hard truths — Tell people the awkward thing, kindly, before they have to find out the hard way.
  • Sponsor others publicly — Amplify the careful, considered voices that aren't yet getting heard.
  • Resist the urge to be omnipresent — Showing up selectively, fully present, beats showing up everywhere half-present.
Wk 1
Notice. No new behaviours. After every meeting, write one sentence: presence or performance?
Wk 2
The pause. Add one habit: the considered pause. Two seconds before any non-trivial answer.
Wk 3
The opening. Deliberate first 30 seconds in every meeting where you're a major voice.
Wk 4
The recovery. A 10-minute recovery window after every demanding meeting.
And after that?

Pick one chapter each month and run it for four weeks. By the end of a year, you will have rebuilt your entire approach to leadership communication.
