Module 01 — W.A.R.M.U.P.™
What this module is about

Before any real work begins, the room has to feel safe enough to take risks.

Most speaking programmes go straight to technique — slides, structure, delivery tips. W.A.R.M.U.P.™ does something different. It creates the psychological conditions that make genuine learning possible in the first place.

This is not about getting comfortable with public speaking in general. It is about becoming comfortable with the specific discomfort of being seen, heard and evaluated — so that the real work can begin.

Barrier addressed

Fear of vulnerability and judgement in group learning settings

The W.A.R.M.U.P.™ framework

Six elements. One foundation.

Each letter addresses a specific component of psychological readiness for high-stakes speaking.

W
Welcome
Creating the conditions where every voice belongs in the room — and where difference becomes a resource rather than a threat.
A
Awareness
Understanding your own patterns of fear and avoidance — where they live in the body, and how they show up before you've said a word.
R
Risk
Taking the first small steps outside your comfort zone — in a space where getting it wrong is part of the method, not a failure of it.
M
Mindset
Shifting from a fixed mindset — "I am not a confident speaker" — to a growth orientation that makes change genuinely possible.
U
Uncomfort
Learning to tolerate and then embrace discomfort as a signal of growth — not as evidence that something is wrong.
P
Play
The professional's secret weapon: approaching practice with curiosity rather than judgement — which is the only state in which real learning happens.
